Azharr

Warrior-Priest of the Twin Moons

Basic Info

Born and raised in the heart of Elsweyr, Azharr considers himself about as Khajiiti as one could reasonably get. Azharr is a prideful creature, possessing a strong will, a fiery soul, and a powerful desire to fight and protect.

Once meant to become spiritual and martial leader of his small clan, Azharr now wanders Tamriel running contracts on behalf of the notorious Fighters Guild, often making only just enough to get by—and drink a little on the side.

Azharr is forward and friendly, naturally supportive of those he’d call allies, and honest by default. He unerringly follows his heart, upholding his honor and pride, and seeking to do good where there is good to be done. Azharr is a protector—to his friends and loved ones, he is fiercely loyal, and he won’t hesitate to put himself in danger to defend the innocent.

The warrior isn’t without his flaws, though. Any personal conflicts might easily be perceived as an affront to his honor—and Azharr does not take this lightly. When he feels his honor has been insulted, he often opts to settle differences through combat, rather than talking things out rationally. Such was the way of his fellow warriors.

As Azharr holds fast to his own code of honor, he often thrusts the same standards upon others, making him come off as overly critical or unfriendly when this may not be his intention at all.

He harbors a natural distrust for other races—Dunmer in particular—but with a little time, he’s often able to overlook his prejudices for those who prove themselves worthy of his trust.

Azharr enjoys testing his combat prowess against those close to him. In his eyes, an honest duel is a most intimate gesture capable of conveying thoughts, feelings, and knowledge beyond words.

Azharr’s first language was Ta’agra, the native language of the Khajiit. His knowledge of the common language is thus quite underdeveloped, and this can easily lead to misunderstandings when communicating with those unfamiliar with the Khajiiti tongue.
